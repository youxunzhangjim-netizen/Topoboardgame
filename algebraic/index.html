Algebraic Rules

Clifford Reversi: place a Pauli-labelled stone on an empty vertex only when it brackets an opponent chain along a topology-aware ray. Bracketed stones flip color; their Pauli labels transform by H or S. Twisted seams can also transport labels.

Pauli labels: X, Y, and Z describe local algebra states. H swaps X and Z while Y stays Y. S maps X to Y, Y to X, and Z stays Z.

Fusion Rules

Anyon Jump: tokens hop to adjacent empty vertices or jump over one occupied neighbor. Jumping over mutually nontrivial toric anyons can add braid tokens. On twist seams, e and m can exchange.

Toric code fusion: e x e = 1, m x m = 1, psi x psi = 1, e x m = psi, e x psi = m, m x psi = e, and 1 x a = a. Vacuum fusion can remove the fused pair.
