Topological Board Game

Same Core Rules. Different Spaces. New Strategy.

TopologicalBoardgame is an interactive platform for classic and physics-inspired board games on changing spaces.

For players, it transforms familiar rules into new strategic worlds: easy to enter, surprising to play, and deep enough to challenge serious competitors.

For researchers, it becomes a visual and playable laboratory for studying how information, locality, symmetry, competition, and physical behavior change when the underlying space changes.

A move is never just a move here. It is a test of the space itself.

Basic Game Books Short beginner notes for players who have not played Chess, Go, Reversi, or Chinese Checkers before. The same basic ideas apply in 2D, 3D, 4D, and spacetime; topology changes the board connections.
Chess

Goal: attack the opponent king so it cannot escape check.

Turn: move one piece. Each piece has its own movement pattern; captures remove the opponent piece on the destination.

Pieces: king one step, queen any straight or diagonal line, rook straight lines, bishop diagonals, knight L jumps, and pawn forward with diagonal capture and promotion.

First habit: protect your king, develop pieces, and control the center or the important topological paths.

Go

Goal: surround more territory while keeping your stones alive.

Turn: place one stone on an empty point or pass. Connected stones share liberties; a group with no liberties is captured.

End: after both players pass and agree count, stones plus surrounded territory, with komi when enabled, decide the winner.

First habit: count liberties, connect weak groups, cut the opponent, and make eyes for life.

Reversi

Goal: finish with more discs of your color than the opponent.

Turn: place a disc so one or more straight lines of opponent discs are bracketed by your discs; bracketed discs flip to your color.

End: pass when you have no legal move. The game ends when the board is full or neither player can move.

First habit: corners and stable edges are powerful. Avoid giving the opponent easy corner moves.

Chinese Checkers

Goal: race your pieces into the target zone across the board.

Turn: step to an adjacent empty point or jump over an occupied neighbor into the empty point beyond it. A jump chain may continue.

Rules: Chinese Checkers has no captures; pieces cannot land on occupied points, and targets follow the selected topology.

First habit: build routes for long chains, keep pieces moving together, and avoid blocking your own exits.
